# Wood Consulting Communications Skill

## Company Structure

**Wood Consulting LLC** is the parent company with four divisions:
- **Capital Energy** — Residential solar & energy sales (D2D)
- **Honest Water** — Water treatment & filtration sales (D2D)
- **D2D Consulting** — Training and consulting for door-to-door sales organizations
- **Recruiting** — Talent acquisition for D2D sales teams

## Comm Type Dispatch Table

| Comm Type | Section | Tone | Length |
|-----------|---------|------|--------|
| Field briefing / morning huddle | A | High-energy, direct, motivational | 150-300 words |
| Division weekly update | B | Professional, data-driven, encouraging | 400-600 words |
| Market expansion memo | C | Strategic, authoritative, forward-looking | 500-800 words |
| Recruiting message / outreach | D | Exciting, aspirational, authentic | 200-400 words |
| Social media / brand post | E | Punchy, visual-friendly, hashtag-ready | 50-200 words |
| External stakeholder update | F | Polished, formal, metrics-oriented | 400-700 words |
| Culture / recognition message | G | Warm, celebratory, personal | 200-400 words |
| Onboarding message | H | Welcoming, clear, action-oriented | 300-500 words |
| General / custom | I | Match request context | Variable |

## Context Gathering

Before writing any communication, determine:
1. **Who is sending it?** (Aryen, division lead, team manager, HR)
2. **Who receives it?** (field reps, managers, investors, recruits, public)
3. **What is the primary action?** (inform, motivate, instruct, celebrate, recruit)
4. **What data/metrics to include?** (doors knocked, close rate, revenue, team size)
5. **What channel?** (Slack, email, text, social, in-app notification)

## Section A — Field Briefings / Morning Huddles

### Template

```
[GREETING — high energy, first-name basis]

TODAY'S FOCUS: [One clear objective]

[2-3 bullet tactical points — what to do differently today]

NUMBERS FROM YESTERDAY:
- [Metric 1]
- [Metric 2]
- [Shoutout to top performer]

[CLOSING — rally cry, 1-2 sentences max]

[Sign-off]
```

### Example

```
What's up team,

TODAY'S FOCUS: Close rate. We're knocking doors, we're getting in — now let's finish.

- Lead with the savings calculator on every pitch. Don't skip it.
- If they say "let me think about it" — that means you haven't shown them the monthly comparison yet.
- Evening doors (5-8pm) are converting 2x right now. Shift your schedule.

YESTERDAY:
- 847 doors knocked across the division
- 12 closes (1.4% — we can hit 2%)
- Marcus hit 4 closes in one shift. Ask him what he's doing different.

Let's have a day. Get after it.

— Aryen
```

### Voice Rules
- Use "we" and "us" — never "you guys"
- Lead with one actionable focus, not a list of everything
- Always include at least one specific name/shoutout
- End with forward momentum, never with a complaint

## Section D — Recruiting Messages

### Template (Cold Outreach)

```
Hey [Name],

[Hook — something specific to them, NOT generic]

I run [Division] at Wood Consulting — we're a [one-line description]. We're expanding into [market/area] and I'm looking for [specific role].

Here's what our top performers are earning: [specific comp range or example]

What makes us different:
- [Differentiator 1 — comp, culture, tech, training]
- [Differentiator 2]
- [Differentiator 3]

[Low-pressure CTA — "worth a 10 min call?" not "apply now"]

[Sign-off]
```

### Voice Rules
- Never say "unlimited earning potential" — give real numbers
- Reference something specific about the person (their LinkedIn, their current role, their location)
- Keep the first message under 200 words
- CTA is always low-friction (call, not application)

```

```
[Big moment opener — capital letters, energy]

[What was achieved — specific numbers]

[Who contributed — name as many people as possible]

[What this means for the team/company]

### Voice Rules
- Be specific — "great job" means nothing without details
- Name individuals — recognition is personal
- Connect the achievement to the bigger mission
- Keep it genuine — forced enthusiasm is worse than none

- Reduce anxiety — first days are overwhelming
- Give clear, checklistable action items
- Provide every resource link/contact they might need
- Set realistic expectations for week 1

## Section I — General / Custom

For any communication that does not fit the above categories:

1. Identify the closest matching section and adapt its template
2. If truly unique, follow these universal rules:
   - Open with the most important information
   - Use bullet points over paragraphs
   - End with a clear action item or next step
   - Match the formality level to the audience

## Universal Quality Bar

Every communication must pass these checks:

1. **One main point** — if you removed everything else, the reader would still get the key message
2. **Action clarity** — the reader knows exactly what to do next (or that no action is needed)
3. **Tone match** — internal casual ≠ external formal ≠ recruiting aspirational
4. **No filler** — every sentence earns its place; delete "I wanted to reach out to let you know that"
5. **Specific over generic** — names, numbers, dates, and details beat vague references
6. **Proofread** — no typos, no broken links, no wrong names
